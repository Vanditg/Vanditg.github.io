About

    My research is in the field of Computer Vision, Deep Learning and Cognitive Vision. Certain questions have led me to this research such as:

    • How human vision works to recognize object/object category with less number of samples?

    • What makes human vision so effective?

    • What can we learn from the human vision?

    • How can we improve object recognition/segmentation with the help of human vision system?

    Thus with the intention of finding the answers, I do research and develop methods for how to use Cognitive and Computer Vision to benefit each other. I collaborate with great minds to understand this research field deeply.

    I have been also working on understanding vision from person’s perspective, such topics include:

    • Activity recognition, Video Classification and Person identification based on the provided description in extremely dense scenes.

    • Human detection, Pose Estimation and Tracking in surveillance vision.

    • Visual Forecasting - Generating Future Prediction for the Scene.

    Therefore in the Past, I have spent time at Ahmedabad University, Meditab Software Inc., Kansas State University, and GTU Robocon Team, where I worked with Hiren Galiyawala, Kenil Shah, Yash Khandhediya, Dr. Prof. Dwight Day, Dr. Prof. Kim Fowler and Prof. Raj Hakani on several projects.

    I am committed to increasing undergraduates participation in the field of Computer Vision and have worked for last two years to motivate undergraduates in computing. To this end, I and my colleague have co-founded Computer Vision Group – LDCE, which focuses on increasing undergraduates knowledge and participation in Computer Vision and Deep Learning Research. Interested to collaborate or join our vision group? Email any of the CVG-LDCE members with the project proposal.
